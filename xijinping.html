Marcos Jr. assures Xi Jinping of PH's ‘partnership, meaningful cooperation’ in region
Katrina Domingo, ABS-CBN News
Published Nov 01, 2025 03:14 PM PHT
GYEONGJU, South Korea — President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. assured Chinese President Xi Jinping of the Philippines’ “partnership and meaningful cooperation” in the region as the two leaders met on the margins of the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation Summit on Saturday.

Marcos Jr. gave details of their short banter where the Filipino leader was seen approaching the Chinese President to shake his hand.

“I congratulated President Xi Jinping as China assumes the APEC Chairmanship in 2026, and reaffirmed the Philippines’ commitment to partnership and meaningful cooperation in our region,” Marcos Jr. said in a social media post.

Malacañang has yet to give further details about the interaction that comes as the Philippines continues to ward off China’s illegal incursions in the West Philippine Sea.

Earlier this week, APEC 2025 host and South Korean President Lee Jae Myung called for “harmony and co-existence” within the 21-member regional bloc, saying that Asia-Pacific economies need to find common ground despite differing national interests for the region to prosper and move forward.

China will host next year’s APEC Summit, while the Philippines will take charge of the 2026 priorities and agenda of the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN).
