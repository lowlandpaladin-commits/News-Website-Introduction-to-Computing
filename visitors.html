Visitors at Manila Memorial Park pay respects to prominent figures
Agatha Gregorio, ABS-CBN News
Published Nov 01, 2025 03:09 PM PHT
The Ninoy and Cory Aquino Foundation, together with Tindig Pilipinas, holds a commemorative Mass at the resting place of former senator Benigno “Ninoy” Aquino Jr. at Manila Memorial Park in Parañaque City on Thursday, August 21, 2025, to mark the 42nd anniversary of his assassination. Maria Tan, ABS-CBN News

MANILA — For many, Undas is a time to commemorate not only departed loved ones, but also prominent figures who made an impact while they were alive. A number of visitors at Manila Memorial Park in Parañaque on November 1 paid respects to prominent figures at the cemetery, including former presidents Cory Aquino and Benigno “Noynoy” Aquino III, and former senator Benigno “Ninoy” Aquino Jr.

Gerald Munda, in particular, came with seven others in his family.

“Usually ‘pag pumupunta kami sa lola namin sa libingan, binibisita rin namin ‘tong puntod ni Pangulong Cory at ni Pangulong Noynoy. Sinasabay namin sa pagbisita,” Munda said.

“Parang ano na sila sa Pilipinas — may nagawa sila sa Pilipinas… Naglingkod sa bayan,” Munda added.

A group of nuns were also seen praying the rosary by the gravesite.

As seen since October 31st, flowers coming from President Ferdinand “Bongbong” Marcos Jr. and the Manila local government were placed by the Aquino family’s tomb.

But on November 1st, more flowers sat in front of the tombs alongside small Philippine flags.

Similarly, a Philippine flag stood on top of slain broadcast journalist Percy Lapid’s tombstone.

It was perched right inside of a white pot of flowers beside some candles.

Also laid to rest at Manila Memorial Park are the late actors AJ Perez and Rico Yan.

Compared to October 31, traffic was noticeably heavier on All Saints’ Day at the Manila Memorial Park.

Around 800,000 visitors are expected at the park for the entire Undas season, according to Manila Memorial Park Assistant Park Manager Len Acosta.

While there were no security checks for vehicles entering the cemetery, bags were checked for those walking in.

A number of items, including six cigarette packs, five lighters, brass knuckles, and two vapes had been confiscated at the entrance since October 31st.

Visitors may claim their confiscated items again upon exit.

The park is open for 24 hours until November 2, but will close at 6 p.m. come November 3.
