Storm surge threatens several provinces as Typhoon Uwan nears land
Dennis Gasgonia, ABS-CBN News
Published Nov 08, 2025 09:40 PM PHT
MANILA - State weather bureau PAGASA warned that there is a high risk of life threatening storm surge in the low-lying coastal communities in the eastern part of the country due to tropical cyclone Uwan within the next 48 hours.

Pagasa said in its 8 pm update on Saturday that the following areas may experience storm surge heights of more than 3 meters:

Albay

Aurora

Cagayan

Camarines Norte

Camarines Sur

Catanduanes

Isabela

Pangasinan

Quezon

Sorsogon

The following areas may experience storm surge heights of 2.1-3 meters:

Cagayan

Camarines Sur

La Union

Northen Samar

Pangasinan

Quezon

Meanwhile, the following areas might experience storm surge heights of 1-2 meters:

Bataan

Bulacan

Cagayan

Cavite

Ilocos Norte

Ilocos Sur

Metro Manila

Pampanga

Zambales

Dinagat Islands

Surigao del Norte

Eastern Samar

Batangas

PAGASA recommended the public living in said areas to take the following actions:

> Stay away from the coast or beach

> Cancel all marine activities

> Move to higher grounds away from the coast and storm surge-prone areas

> Be aware and follow latest updates from DOST-PAGASA

Typhoon Uwan continued to rapidly intensify Saturday afternoon as it moved closer towards the Philippine Sea east of the Bicol Region, PAGASA said.

The center of the typhoon's eye may pass close to Catanduanes Sunday morning and make landfall over the southern portion of Isabela or the northern portion of Aurora Sunday evening or Monday early morning, the state weather bureau added.

PAGASA has raised wind signal no. 3 over Catanduanes, eastern Camarines Sur, eastern Albay, northeastern Sorsogon, and northeastern Northern Samar.

Meanwhile, Metro Manila and 19 others areas in eastern Luzon and Eastern Visayas are now under signal no. 2 while 41 other areas in Luzon, Visayas, and Caraga have been placed under signal no. 1.
