Task Force Philippines to ‘converge’ PH’s bilateral activities – DND
Job Manahan, ABS-CBN News
Published Nov 02, 2025 08:59 PM PHT
MANILA – Defense Secretary Gilbert Teodoro on Sunday said Task Force Philippines was meant to converge Manila’s activities with other countries, just as it would also provide assistance when the country gets struck by natural calamities.

Last week, the Philippines and the United States launched the task force, which is supposedly designed to strengthen cooperation against security threats and acts of aggression in the Indo-Pacific region.

US Secretary of War Pete Hegseth explained that this seeks to improve “interoperability” and “preparedness for contingencies” between the two militaries so they may “decisively respond to crisis or aggression and re-establish deterrence in the South China Sea.”

But in an interview following the signing of the Status of Visiting Forces Agreement (SOVFA) with Canada today, Teodoro said the task force is also an “operational engagement” between Manila and Washington’s military forces.

Armed Forces of the Philippines chief-of-staff Gen. Romeo Brawner, Jr., has already begun the strategic command as part of the task force, according to the defense chief.

“The task force [converges] all other bilateral activities,” Teodoro told reporters when sought for comments on the task force.

“The task force has a specific mission and that mission is to converge with our resilience both internally and externally and it is a–shall we say an efficiency mechanism to funnel all the activities,” he added.

“This task force will put more regularity in an immediate response to the natural calamities that we are facing,” he said.

The task force, he said, falls under the purview of the Mutual Defense Treaty with the US and not the Visiting Forces Agreement.

“All these task forces under the different articles of the Mutual Defense Treaty… that is the basis of the task force. It is nothing new but an operating agreement… raised up to our level,” he said.

This Saturday, President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. said that he does not expect the task force to heighten tensions in the West Philippine Sea.

"I hope it will lower the tensions in the West Philippine Sea, certainly not heighten them because it is not something new… Nagkaka-heightening lang ng tension kapag may sumubok ng bago and that's not what's going to happen," the President had said.
