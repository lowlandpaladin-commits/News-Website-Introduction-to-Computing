PH needs to ‘take advantage’ of AI, Marcos Jr. says
Katrina Domingo, ABS-CBN News
Published Nov 01, 2025 11:55 PM PHT
BUSAN, South Korea — The Philippines needs to “take advantage” of artificial intelligence (AI) “as much as we can” and “as soon as we can” as technology rapidly changes, President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. said on Saturday, after Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC) leaders tackled concerns over the responsible and fair use of AI.

Under the APEC 2025 Leaders’ Declaration, member economies of the regional bloc emphasized the necessity of “continued efforts to enhance, security, trustworthiness, accessibility, and reliability in realizing the benefits of AI.”

The Philippines needs to adopt AI in government “as much as we can, as soon as we can,” Marcos Jr. said in a press conference here.

“You're missing a chance if you wait. AI is going to come, parang alon yan eh. Kahit anong gawin mo, mababasa ka, and if you don't learn how to swim, if you do not learn how to use AI in the best way, and in a secure way, and in a benevolent way, may iwanan ka talaga,” he said.

“It's such a powerful tool. You must take advantage of it as quickly as possible. You have to learn how to use it as quickly as possible,” he said.

Other APEC leaders are doing the same thing as well, the President said.

“AI will overwhelm us if we do not learn how to swim with the tide, if we do not learn how to handle it properly, if we do not learn how we can, how we can use it to best help our people,” he said.

“There's so many aspects to it that it really has to be studied very, very hard.”

So far, the Philippine government is still in the process of consulting think tanks on where the AI technology is headed to, and how countries can make the most out of it.

Earlier this week, Marcos Jr. invited investors to do more business in the Philippines, claiming that the government has been closely collaborating with the private sector to make the country future-ready.
