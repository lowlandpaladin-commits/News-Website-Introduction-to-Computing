Signal No. 5 raised in several areas as 'Uwan' intensifies to super typhoon
Ariel Rojas, ABS-CBN News
Published Nov 09, 2025 06:46 AM PHT | Updated Nov 09, 2025 08:55 AM PHT
MANILA (UPDATED) — 'Uwan' intensified into a super typhoon on Sunday morning bringing life-threatening conditions in Catanduanes.

Wind signal no. 5 is up over Polillo Islands, northern Camarines Norte, eastern Camarines Sur, and Catanduanes.

Violent winds are expected to bring extreme threat to life and property in these areas.

Wind Signal No. 4 meanwhile is hoisted over eastern Quezon, the rest of Camarines Norte, the rest of Camarines Sur, and eastern Albay.

At 7 a.m. Sunday, super typhoon Uwan was located 125 km east northeast of Virac, Catanduanes. It is now packing winds of 185 kph and gusts up to 230 kph.

It is moving west northwestward at 25 kph.

Uwan’s eyewall is grazing the northern portion of Catanduanes bringing catastrophic winds in the area.

It may make landfall over Aurora tonight or early Monday at or near peak intensity of 195 kph winds.

It may weaken to a typhoon while crossing mountainous Northern Luzon.

The storm will emerge over the West Philippine Sea Monday morning.

While recurving, Uwan will exit the Philippine area of responsibility on Tuesday. It will re-enter the northwestern boundary by Thursday, cross Taiwan, and exit the PAR for good on Friday.
