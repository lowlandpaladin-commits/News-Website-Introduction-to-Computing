JMA: Typhoon Uwan may devastate Aurora, Isabela, Northern Catanduanes
Jojo Pasion Malig, ABS-CBN News
Published Nov 08, 2025 08:29 PM PHT | Updated Nov 08, 2025 08:35 PM PHT
MANILA - The Philippine provinces of Aurora, Isabela, and northern Catanduanes face the highest risk of devastation from Typhoon Uwan (international name Fung-wong), the Japan Meteorological Agency (JMA) warned on Saturday.

The Japanese weather agency, which is one of the Regional Specialized Meteorological Centers designated by the World Meteorological Organization (WMO), said that the three areas, which are in the path of the typhoon, may experience "destructive to devastating winds and torrential rainfall" that will peak late Saturday through Sunday afternoon due to the intensifying core of the tropical cyclone.

The JMA also warned that the coastal areas of Aurora and Isabela are under an "extremely high threat of life-threatening storm surge."

It said the Ilocos Region and La Union also face the highest risk of destructive winds and torrential rainfall, peaking late Sunday night through Monday afternoon as the typhoon's core passes just over the West Philippine Sea. The coastal areas are also under high threat of storm surge.

The JMA said Ilocos Norte remains within the high-risk zone, with the typhoon center expected to pass west northwest of Laoag City on Tuesday afternoon.

Meanwhile, Central Luzon and the Cordillera Administrative Region may experience severe winds and intense rainfall on Sunday and Monday. Communities in the the Cordilleras also face an extreme threat of landslides and flash floods from intense enhanced rainfall.

The JMA expects Typhoon Uwan to continue intensifying over the warm waters of the Philippine Sea and reach its peak intensity in 24 hours, before the tropical cyclone's eye makes its landfall south-southeast of Casiguran, Aurora late Sunday night.

"The track maintains a high threat of destructive winds and severe flooding across Central and Northern Luzon," JMA warned.

The US Joint Typhoon Warning Center, in its latest typhoon advisory on Saturday, also expects Uwan to make landfall just south of Casiguran, Aurora.

The JTWC said Uwan may reach peak intensity of around 203 kph 1-minute maximum sustained winds, with gusts of more than 240kph by Sunday.

It said the tropical cyclone will continue on a northwestward track, with only high vertical wind shear slowing down its intensification process as it approaches luzon and makes landfall over Luzon.

Philippine state weather bureau PAGASA on Saturday said Uwan may reach super typhoon category Saturday night or Sunday, and wind signal no. 5 may be raised in areas that will be hit by the tropical cyclone.

PAGASA has raised wind signal no. 3 over Catanduanes, eastern Camarines Sur, eastern Albay, northeastern Sorsogon, and northeastern Northern Samar.

Meanwhile, Metro Manila and 19 others areas in eastern Luzon and Eastern Visayas are now under signal no. 2 while 41 other areas in Luzon, Visayas, and Caraga have been placed under signal no. 1.
